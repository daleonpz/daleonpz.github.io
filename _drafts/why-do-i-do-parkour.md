All my values and philosophy are based on my parkour experiences. The way I see the world and how I live is the result of every jump I did, every fear I overcame and every person I met along the way. Since 2011 I have been doing parkour and I want to do it as long as I can. However, I was looking for something before that year...

### Act 1:
As a teenager I had some emotional issues, I had low self-esteem, my relations were chaotic and I felt like that I didn't belong anywhere, even I sometimes thought about suicide and hurting myself just because I used to believe that I deserved it. Of course, there were no reason for that now that I think about it, but that was how I used to live. Anyway, on the hand, since my younger years I wanted to be like the superheros you watched in TV, it seemed that these character were fearless, overwhelming, badass and the most important attribute was that they were smiling and in peace with themselves. It didn't matter how difficult the challenge was, they always faced it and overcame after few tries and a lot of will power. 

### Act 2:
When I was eighteen I enrolled in tae-kwon-do near my university in Lima, this was my first milestone, my first step to becoming the person I wanted to be. I wanted to be stronger, because I believed that the strongest person is the happiest one due to the strongest will endure a difficult live (to quote Bruce Lee) and remain in peace. Thus, martial arts was obvious path, it required discipline, hard work, patience, dealing with frustation and facing the fear of figthing against someone stronger, bigger and maybe more proefficient than you. Even though, I had more confident in myself after two years of training, I felt something was missing, I was still having self destructive tendency.
