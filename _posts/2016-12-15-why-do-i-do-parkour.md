All my values and philosophy are based on my parkour experiences. The way I see the world and how I live are the result of every jump I did, every fear I overcame and every person I met along the way. Since 2011 I have been doing parkour and I want to do it as long as I can. However, everything started years before...

### Act 1:
As a teenager I had some emotional issues, I had low self-esteem, my relations were chaotic and I felt like that I didn't belong anywhere, even I sometimes thought about suicide and hurting myself just because I used to believe that I deserved it. Of course, there were no reason for that now that I think about it, but that was how I used to live. Anyway...
On the other hand, since my younger years I wanted to be like the superheros you saw on TV, it seemed that these characters were fearless, overwhelming, badass and the most important attributes were that they were smiling and in peace with themselves. It didn't matter how difficult the challenge was, at the end, they always faced and overcame it after a lot of hardwork and will power. 

### Act 2:
When I was eighteen I enrolled in tae-kwon-do near my university in Lima, this was my first milestone, my first step to become the person I wanted to be. I wanted to be stronger, because I believed that the strongest person is the happiest one due to the strongest will endure a difficult live (to quote Bruce Lee) and remain in peace. Thus, martial arts was the obvious path, it required discipline, hard work, patience, dealing with frustation and facing the fear of figthing against someone stronger, bigger and maybe more proefficient than you. Even though, I had more confident in myself after two years of training, I felt something was missing, I was still having a self destructive tendency.  

## Act 3:
I used to spend a lot of time sleeping or just laying on my bed watching TV or youtube vids, I am still doing it but let's keep our focus on the story.
One day I turned on the TV and I saw a parkour video in NatGeo, of course at that moment I didn't know anything about parkour. 
That video impressed me and three thougths came up, in the following order: "You need to be very brave to do that shit", "you must be very strong to be able to perform those jumps" and "it's so cool!". 

I kept watching the TV show "Fight Science", by the way the guys that performed on the show were Daniel Ilabaca and Ryan Doyle. In some part of the program the announcer mentioned the word "parkour", so after watching the show I did my reseach and found a scene from the "Yamakasi" first movie that made me feel free. From that day I haven't stopped doing parkour.

## Act 4:
From every jump I did, every fear I overcame and every person I met, I developed my own understanding about life and, most important, myself. 
I was able to face myself. When everything was dark and you were alone and started listening to your own thoughts, and you felt fear of them, in that moment, I chose to accept my past, accept my self-destructive tendency and accept that shit happens but above all things, I realized that when you are in the bottom of the hole, you have two options: climb it  or die, so I chose to climb it. 

Now I am in peace with me, my self-esteem have improved a lot,  and I am not afraid of being hurt, because if something ever happen I just need to overcome that obstacle. 
I learned from parkour that every obstacle is a reflection of you and only you have the power to jump over and keep going forward . 

At the end, I do parkour because I want to be strong to be <b>happy</b>.

It's also cool!
