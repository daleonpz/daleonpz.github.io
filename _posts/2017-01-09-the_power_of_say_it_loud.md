Sometimes when I am jumping around I have been in the situation where there is an obstacle that is totally achievable or doable but something within me makes me hesitate.
Even though, I know that I can make the jump, not only because I have a deep understanding of the technique, but also I have the physical attributes that allows me to accomplish the jump, I still have fear.
New circumstances may be triggered this fear.
The jump is higher or farther, or maybe you want to do it on a rail. 


But... when you start to think whether you can actually make it, it is the moment when you let negative thoughts get into your mind.
"It's really far, I don't sure ANYMORE if I can make it", "what will happen if I don't have enough power... if I slip... if it hurts... ", "Am I ready for this?", " maybe another day", "my body want to do it but my mind is holding me back".
It's okay to have fear and some doubts, because it is a new experience. 
Fear is not bad, it makes you think about whether you are fully aware of what you are about to do.
However, it is up to you how to deal with fear. 

Every time I face an uncomfortable and/or scary situation, but I know that it is doable at my current level, these negative thoughts start messing up my mind and focus.
I realized that saying something loud and clear might counter the negative thoughts.
Usually, I say <b>"que paja debe sentirse lograr esto"</b>, in English it will be  "It will feel so cool to make this jump", and the more I say it the more confident and calm I feel.
After a moment, I start  smiling and I feel how the energy and power is flowing through my body, and this is when everything  happens.
I prepared myself for the jump and go for it 100 percent.


Once you  made jump, you realized that it wasn't a big deal.
Everything was in your mind. 
Of course, you always wish that these kind of fears won't ever happen again. On the other hand, uncomfortable situations make you feel alive.
So, whenever you face any type of challenge, mental or physical, reminder yourself why you wanted to do it, and how incredible it is to achieve that goal.
