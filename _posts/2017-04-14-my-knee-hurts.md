I have suffered from patellar tendonitis for almost 2 years, actually it is called tendinosis, if you have pain for a long period of time. It could be from 2-5 weeks up to several years. In other words, tendinosis isa chronic stage, while tendonitis is an acute stage. 
You develop patellar tendinosis by ignoring the symptoms of patellar tendonitis [Total Knee Health](https://www.amazon.com/Beating-Patellar-Tendonitis-Martin-Koban-ebook/dp/B00F9210UI).

Some reasons I thought were causing my tendinopathy.
* Too much training
* The shoes I was wearing
* My feet structure
* My knees were weak
* Bad landing

Some one them were true. 
I checked my knees and did my rehab but my patellar tendopathy came back again. 
It could be the right or left knee, and sometimes both of them. 

Thus, in this post I will try to sumarize all the information I gathered based on the books I read and my onw experiences. 
I will focused mainly in patellar tendonitis, but the concepts I will explain can be apply to any other tendopathy. 

You should learn and remember the following statements:

First:
:   "If your knee hurts, it is most likely that the problem is not the knee."

-----

Check mobility, stability and imbalances: ankle, hip, spine, good quality tissue.

Second:
:   "Train hard, but most important, train smart."

-----

Train hard it good, but don't be reckless. Have training log. Don't overtrain. Learn the proper technique. 

Third:
:   "If it doesn't hurt, it doesn't mean that it is not injured."

-----

Pain it a symptom that something has passed a threshold. And it is already damage. 

Fourth:
:   "Training also means rehab"

-----

rehab shoulh be part of your training to prevent or recovery from injuries
